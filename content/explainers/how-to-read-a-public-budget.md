A public budget is a map of priorities. Start with three questions: where does
the money come from, where does it go, and what changed from the previous year?

Then look for the everyday effects. A budget can affect bus frequency, school
staffing, permitting speed, library hours, public safety staffing, property tax
bills, and the maintenance backlog for roads and utilities.
